# Cello Bow Distribution and Phrasing Principles

Instrument: Cello
Topic: Bow distribution, phrasing
License: Original test content created for MusicianLog

## Long Romantic Phrases
When practicing long Romantic phrases (such as the primary theme in Dvořák's Cello Concerto, 1st movement), the most important principle is uneven bow distribution. You must allocate more bow speed for the peak of the phrase and save bow for the less intense notes. 

## Unstable Vibrato
If vibrato becomes unstable, especially on the 4th finger, it is often due to a collapsed knuckle joint or excessive tension in the thumb. Ensure the finger joints remain curved and the thumb lightly touches the neck without squeezing. Practice without the instrument first to isolate the rolling motion.

## Actionable Practice
1. Play the passage slowly without vibrato to check intonation and pure bow sound.
2. Alter the rhythm (dotted rhythms, triplets) to strengthen finger articulation.
3. Gradually reintroduce vibrato and piece back the original phrase.
